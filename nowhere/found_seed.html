Found

by Zara John

This seed has travelled up and down the esophagus of a bird. We found it under a nest in the forest below Mount Kinabalu on the island of Borneo, in Sabah, Malaysia. I was working as a field biologist, pushing and pulling my way through that spiny forest in search of bird nests. The seeds pile up under the hanging mossy nests with the Whitehead’s Broadbill tucked inside, keeping her eggs warm, digesting fruit and regurgitating the seeds. The Whitehead’s Broadbill is an insane neon green and makes noises that sound like a transistor radio. They exist only on the slopes of Mount Kinabalu. Once, one flew straight at me, as if I wasn’t there, and saw me at the last minute; neon kamikaze. Older adult humans in spotless quick-dry clothing spend days trying to see this bird. We kept our lips sealed and avoided the bumbling groups accentuated by Swarovski binoculars. The seed has grown polished from the oils of my hand. I hold it in remembrance of days WHEN green had no limit.


ZARA JOHN is a liberal arts graduate turned field ornithologist. After graduating with a degree in English literature, the lure of forests and far away lands led her to spend the past five years in pursuit of birds and their habits. Now, she is back in school, writing again, but her field biologist inclinations haven't left her. She knows she'll be back one day soon.
