Notebook

This page is from March 28 when I was back in New York for a conference. I lived in New York for a long time. I was sitting in a French restaurant in midtown called la Bonne Soupe; it was one of my favorite places when I was younger. So I went back to have lunch – steak hache, sauce maison (a delicious mushroom and wine concoction) – and to survey the scene. Lots of gray-haired old men with glasses of red wine. The place had been updated: the walls were now cream with modern silver sconces. The walls used to be a worn-out red, I think, with vintage light fixtures like Belle Époque Paris. My waitress said there had been a fire in 2009, so they had to redo a lot. I felt very much at home but also out of time, or in another time. I wrote this page in the past tense, which is strange. —Susan Harlan
