Front End Developer

If you want to code the future of publishing, join us

Publet is creating the publishing platform for narrative content on the Web. We give storytellers the tools to be professional media companies and power business models for narrative content (NYT's Snowfall is just the beginning).

As a lead front end engineer at Publet, you’ll be coding a SaaS tool to create responsive, multimedia publications for tablet, phone and desktop. You'll be joining a team with stellar UX and HTML5/JS/CSS3 expertise and a passion for giving the new generation of storytellers a seamless publishing experience. Some skills you'll need include:

  • Rock solid JavaScript, HTML5 and CSS3 experience. AngularJS preferred.
  • Familiarity building on Django templates.
  • Clean visual aesthetic to translate best design practices for the responsive web.
  • Mastery of cross browser compatibility.

    • Publet comes with all the risks, perks, and problem solving of a startup, as well as the company of a world-class editorial, design and development team. We write and design for national publications (FastCompany, The Economist, Rolling Stone), officiate the Awesome Foundation, Escape from Alcatraz and teach code to journalists. We have fun and think big. Join us. Shoot us an email with a short note and the projects, code or achievements that represent you best. The position comes with a salary between $60-$100K plus equity based on experience and commitment.

Designer

If you want to design the future of publishing, join us

Publet is looking for a highly-motivated art director with editorial experience to lead the company’s RESPONSIVE DESIGN practice.

Publet creates publishing tools for narrative content on the Web. The art director will be designing an easy-to-read and typographically-sound user experience so audiences can consume multimedia stories on tablets, phones and the desktop. The art director will inform UI design to create these stories, as well as the design options for creators. The job combines stellar design and smart editorial thinking with a passion for giving storytellers the best possible tools across the Web. Qualifications include:

  • A strong editorial background, and the ability to translate traditional editorial conventions to the digital realm
  • A refined, modern, clean visual aesthetic
  • Full commitment to the promise of responsive design
  • A strong interest in UI design
  • An understanding of Web type and related licensing issues
  • The ability to stay organized while juggling multiple, complicated projects
  • Photoshop chops are a must
  • Some knowledge of coding would be helpful

    • The art director will be working with a team based primarily in the Bay Area, New York and remote locations, so she or he must be able to work independently. The job will begin with discrete projects and ramp up to a permanent position within several months as the product nears launch.

    This is a paid position with the possibility of equity depending on experience and commitment. This job is with a young design and technology start-up which brings all the risks, and perks, associated with being a young, growing company. Please send your resume, portfolio and, in lieu of a cover letter, a graphic or five sentences about why you’re applying.
